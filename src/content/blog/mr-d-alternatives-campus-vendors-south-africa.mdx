## Why campus vendors are reconsidering Mr D

Mr D and Uber Eats were built for a specific use case: connecting restaurants to customers spread across a city, using a driver fleet to cover the distance. That infrastructure has real costs, and the 25–35% commission funds it.

The problem for campus vendors is that the same commission rate applies when the customer is 200 metres away. There's no driver required for a walk-up campus order — but you're paying for one anyway.

On R4,000 in daily campus orders, that's **R1,080–R1,400 in commission per day** — or roughly **R270,000–R350,000 per year** — before you've covered ingredients, staff, or rent.

### 2. SnapScan / Yoco direct ordering

**Best for:** Very small vendors who want digital payment without a platform

**Commission:** Payment processing only (~2.95% + VAT on Yoco)

**Cost:** Free to set up

Some campus vendors use Yoco or SnapScan as a standalone payment tool — students scan a QR code, pay, and show their phone at the counter. There's no ordering system, no queue management, no pickup codes — it's just replacing cash with digital payment.

**Honest limitation:** This is a payment solution, not an ordering solution. It doesn't solve queue management, order tracking, pre-ordering, or analytics. For a high-volume campus vendor, it creates the same counter chaos as walk-up cash orders.

---

### 3. WhatsApp ordering + Yoco payment

**Best for:** Very low volume vendors who know their customers personally

**Commission:** Zero (Yoco processing only)

**Cost:** Free

Some campus vendors take orders via WhatsApp — students message their order, vendor confirms, they pay via Yoco link or SnapScan on collection. This is the DIY version of pre-ordering.

**Honest limitation:** It doesn't scale. WhatsApp order management at volume is genuinely chaotic — missed messages, wrong orders, no queue visibility, no pickup codes. It works for 10–20 orders a day. At 50+, it breaks.

---

### 4. Build your own ordering system

**Best for:** Larger campus operations with dev resources or budget

**Commission:** Zero (Yoco or payment processor of choice)

**Cost:** R5,000–R50,000+ depending on scope

Some larger campus vendors (university dining services, franchise operations) build or commission custom ordering apps. You own the system entirely, control the UX, and pay no platform commission.

**Honest limitation:** High upfront cost, ongoing maintenance, and you're responsible for getting students to download your specific app. Campus-native apps like uTap have a distribution advantage — students already use it for their student card, so the ordering feature is a natural extension rather than a cold download.
