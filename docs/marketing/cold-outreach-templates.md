*For: NMU SRC, campus vendors, and university partnership teams*
*Tone: Direct, honest, not salesy. We ask for small things first.*

## 2. Campus Vendor — uShop onboarding

**Context:** This is a cold approach to a vendor who doesn't know uTap yet. Keep it extremely short. Vendors are busy. The goal of this email is a reply — not a yes. The conversation closes in person.

**Send to:** Vendor owner or manager (get the right contact from staff on site, not a generic info email)  
**When:** During vendor seeding phase (Phase 2)

**Option A — Email (if you have their address):**

---

Subject: Commission-free campus ordering for [Vendor Name]

Hi [Name],

I'm reaching out about a campus ordering app we're launching at NMU.

Quick version: students order ahead from your store, pay you the menu price, and collect with a pickup code. We don't take a commission — you keep what you earn.

Setup takes 15 minutes. There's no contract and no monthly fee to start.

Would you be open to a quick visit so I can show you how it works on your device?

[Founder Name]  
uTap · hello@utaptech.co.za

---

**Option B — WhatsApp (preferred for most campus vendors):**

> "Hi [Name], I'm [Founder Name] from uTap — we're launching a campus ordering app at NMU. Students order ahead, pay menu price, collect with a code. We take zero commission. Setup is 15 minutes. Would you be open for me to pop in and show you how it works?"

**In-person script (once you're there):**

> "We're launching a campus ordering app at NMU. It takes 15 minutes to set up. You list for free. We don't take a commission — students pay you the menu price and collect with a pickup code. Want to try it before we open to all students?"

**Objection handling:**

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I already use Mr D" | "Great — keep using it for delivery orders. uTap is for walk-up and pickup. Different customer behaviour, zero commission. They're not competing." |
| "How do students pay?" | "Through the app via card or wallet. Processed by Yoco — same as if they tapped at your counter. You get paid the same day." |
| "What if no students use it?" | "You're not losing anything. The listing is free and the setup takes 15 minutes. If orders come, you process them. If they don't, no cost to you." |
| "Will students actually download it?" | "The card wallet is why students download it — not the food ordering. Every student needs their campus card. Once the card is in the app, the ordering feature is right there." |

**When:** After NMU data is available (post-launch, Week 6–8)

---

I'm writing to introduce uTap and to explore whether [University Name] would consider a formal partnership for the 2026 academic year.

**What uTap does**

uTap is a campus digital wallet that allows students to use their NFC student card via their smartphone. Students scan their existing MIFARE-standard card once, and from that point use their phone at campus access points exactly as they would the physical card — no hardware changes required on the institution's side.

The platform also includes:
- **uShop**: a pickup-first campus ordering system that connects students to campus vendors at zero delivery fee and zero vendor commission
- **uGig**: student event discovery and access management
- **Cloud-backed credentials**: if a student loses their phone, their card is restored to a new device after identity verification — eliminating lost-card administration costs

**Our current deployment**

2. A soft endorsement in student communications (optional but effective)

## Outreach Sequencing (Recommended Order)

```
Week -3: Approach SRC (longest lead time — they need internal approval)
Week -2: Approach top 5 vendors (need setup time before launch)
Week -2: Approach student media (need 48h for exclusive to matter)
Week -1: Approach additional vendors
Week -1: Send university partnership email to next 3 target campuses
Launch:  SRC co-posts, student media story goes live
Week +6: Follow up university partnership with NMU data
```

**Golden rule:** Always ask for the smallest possible commitment in the first contact. The goal of the first email is a reply, not a yes.
