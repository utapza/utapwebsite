# uTap Social Media Strategy

*Created: May 2026 | Owner: Shared (Aya / Lutho / Blessing rotating) | Capacity: 1–2 posts per week*

---

## The Honest Starting Point

uTap has zero social media accounts today. That is not a crisis — it is a clean slate. A bad account with no strategy is worse than no account. What we are building here is a lean, sustainable presence that a 3-person pre-launch team can actually maintain without it becoming a burden.

The rule for everything in this document: **if we cannot sustain it at 1–2 posts per week, we do not plan for it.**

## Phase 2 — Platform Strategy

### Why These Platforms in This Order

**Instagram first** because it is where South African students aged 18–25 spend the most time, it is a visual platform that suits campus life content, and Reels give organic reach to non-followers — critical when you have zero followers to start.

**TikTok first** alongside Instagram because a single viral NFC demo video or campus launch clip can do more in 48 hours than 3 months of Twitter threads. The NMU physical launch is a TikTok-native moment.

**X second** because it is where SA tech founders, vendors, and journalists are — not primarily students. It builds credibility and press relationships, but it will not drive your first 1,000 downloads. It is the B2B and thought leadership channel.

**LinkedIn second** because it is the right channel for vendor acquisition, university partnerships, and the B2B advertising pitch. Not for students.

**Facebook third** because it is where NMU student group communities actually live. The NMU class groups, res groups, and buy-and-sell groups are on Facebook. You do not need a brand page to participate in those groups, but you need one to be legitimate when students look you up.

## Phase 3 — Content Strategy Per Platform

### Instagram

**Primary audience:** SA university students 18–25

**Content mix at 1–2 posts per week:**
- 50% Reels (15–30 seconds) — highest organic reach
- 30% Carousels — educational, swipeable, shareable
- 20% Single image posts — quotes, data stats, campus photography

**Content types that work:**

*Reels:*
- NFC card scan demo — phone taps card, animation shows it loading into the app (30 seconds, no talking needed, just satisfying tap + visual)
- "What you actually pay" — quick split-screen of Mr D total vs uTap total on same R80 order
- Behind the build — 30-second clip of the team, the product, the NMU trip prep
- Campus vendor intro — 20-second clip of a vendor at their stall with uTap pickup code on screen
- "Day in the life with uTap" — student POV: tap gate, order lunch, collect with code

*Carousels:*
- "The R100 campus meal breakdown" — slide 1: student pays R100 / slides 2–4: where the money goes on Mr D / slide 5: where it goes on uTap / slide 6: download CTA
- "Your student card is just an NFC chip" — explainer on what MIFARE is and why your phone can replace it
- "5 things costing you money at university" — delivery fees is slide 1, card replacement is slide 3

*Single images:*
- Stat graphics: "South African students spend R636/month on delivery fees. On campus." Clean, bold typography.
- Quote graphics: "Your student card lives on your phone now." Campus background.
- Launch countdown: "NMU is first. [X] weeks away."

**Caption formula:**
- Line 1: hook (surprising stat, bold claim, or question)
- Lines 2–4: the point, fast
- Line 5: CTA ("Download free — link in bio" or "Share this with someone paying R40 delivery fees")
- Hashtags (5–8, not 30): #SouthAfricanStudents #CampusLife #NMU #StudentLife #uTap #NoDeliveryFees

**Posting cadence:** 1 post per week minimum. During NMU launch week: daily.

### TikTok

**Primary audience:** SA university students 18–22 — the youngest, highest-energy segment

**Content mix at 1 post per week:**
- 70% authentic, low-production short videos (15–60 seconds)
- 30% trending audio + campus content overlay

**Content types that work:**

*High priority:*
- NFC tap demo — the most satisfying product demo possible. Phone to card. Tap. Student card appears on screen. 15 seconds. No voiceover needed.
- "POV: you ordered lunch before your lecture ended" — student walks out, collects food, walks past the queue. 20 seconds.
- "We're launching at NMU and here's what happened" — day 1 of the PE trip, raw footage, honest
- Vendor setup in 15 minutes — timelapse of a vendor going from WhatsApp to live store
- "How much delivery apps take from your vendor" — point the camera at the maths on a whiteboard

*What to avoid on TikTok:*
- Overly produced content — it performs worse than authentic
- Corporate tone — kills engagement instantly
- Posting and disappearing — TikTok rewards consistency and engagement in comments

**TikTok-specific tactic:** Reply to comments with video replies. If someone comments "which campuses?" reply with a 15-second video saying which campuses are coming. TikTok surfaces these heavily.

**Posting cadence:** 1 post per week. During NMU launch: every day of the trip.

### X / Twitter

**Primary audience:** SA tech founders, journalists, vendors, campus administrators, startup community

**Content mix at 1 post per week:**
- 60% threads (data-driven, founder perspective, campus commerce insights)
- 40% standalone tweets (hot takes, quick stats, replies that build presence)

**Thread formula that works for uTap:**
1. Hook tweet: the most surprising or provocative version of the point
2. Expand with 4–6 numbered points, each adding something new
3. Close with the product tie-in — never lead with it
4. Pin a reply asking a question to generate engagement

**Standalone tweet formula:**
Under 140 characters. One idea. One surprise. No hashtags (they look desperate on X unless trending).

**The daily X habit (15 minutes):**
Reply to 3–5 accounts in the SA startup / student / campus space with genuine insight. This builds followers faster than posting alone. Target accounts: @TechCentral_SA, @VentureBurnSA, student union accounts, SA university official accounts, Yoco, campus food vendors with a presence.

**Posting cadence:** 1 thread per week + daily engagement (replies, not posts).

---

### LinkedIn

**Primary audience:** Campus food vendors, university administrators, SA tech press, potential B2B partners

**Content mix at 1 post per week:**
- 40% vendor economics posts (the margin maths)
- 30% campus commerce thought leadership
- 20% product and company updates
- 10% B2B / data advertising angle

**LinkedIn-specific rules:**
- No external links in the post body — put them in the first comment
- First line must be a hook that stops the scroll — LinkedIn cuts off at 2–3 lines
- End with a question to drive comments (LinkedIn algorithm rewards comment activity)
- Tag relevant people sparingly — only when genuinely relevant

**Best-performing LinkedIn formats for uTap:**
- Numbered list posts: "3 things campus vendors get wrong about delivery apps"
- The maths post: R100 order → what each platform takes → what you keep
- Founder story: honest, short, first-person — what we got wrong building uTap
- Milestone posts: "uTap is live at NMU" — numbers, lessons, what's next

**Posting cadence:** 1 post per week.

## Phase 4 — Ownership & Rotation Model

With a 3-person team at 1–2 posts per week, the rotating model works as follows:

### Week Rotation

| Week | Instagram + TikTok | X Thread | LinkedIn |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week A | Aya creates + posts | Lutho drafts, Aya approves | Blessing drafts, Aya approves |
| Week B | Lutho creates + posts | Blessing drafts, Aya approves | Aya drafts + posts |
| Week C | Blessing creates + posts | Aya drafts + posts | Lutho drafts, Aya approves |

**Aya has final approval on everything before it posts.** Brand voice consistency requires one person as the editorial filter. This is not about control — it is about making sure the brand sounds like one person, not three.

**Content creation does not need to happen in the same week as posting.** Build a 2-week buffer of drafted posts. When someone has a good idea, they draft it and put it in the buffer. The person whose week it is picks from the buffer or creates fresh.

### The Buffer System
Create a shared Google Doc or Notion page titled "uTap Post Buffer" with three sections:
- Ready to post (approved, needs only scheduling)
- Needs review (drafted, not yet approved)
- Ideas (rough concepts, not yet written)

Target: always have at least 4 posts in "Ready to post" so no week is scrambling.

## Phase 5 — NMU Launch Week Social Plan

The NMU trip is your single best content opportunity before the Beta. Lutho and Blessing will be on the ground — that is raw, authentic content that no amount of studio production can replicate.

### What to capture during the PE trip

**Day 1:**
- Short clip of arriving on campus (15 seconds, no talking)
- First vendor conversation — not the actual pitch, but the moment after: "Vendor 1 is in." Text on screen.
- The NMU campus itself — any iconic spot students would recognise

**Day 2:**
- Hands-on store setup — timelapse or fast cut of photographing menu items, setting up the listing
- The first test order going through — phone screen recording of the pickup code appearing
- Vendor reaction when the test order works — genuine, unscripted

**Day 3:**
- Class talk footage if the lecturer permits — even just the room, not necessarily faces
- First students scanning their card — 10-second clip of the NFC tap
- The vendor counter during a busy period — the queue, the pickup codes, the energy

**Post-trip:**
- "We're back in Joburg. Here's what happened." — honest debrief thread on X + carousel on Instagram

### NMU Launch Week Posting Schedule

| Day | Instagram | TikTok | X | LinkedIn |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1 (arrive PE) | Story: "We're in PE 🇿🇦" | "Day 1 of launching uTap at NMU" raw clip | "We're in Port Elizabeth. uTap goes live at NMU this week." | — |
| Day 2 (vendor setup) | Reel: vendor store setup | Timelapse of store setup | "Vendor 1 is live. [Name of store] is now on uTap uShop." | "What vendor onboarding looks like in person" |
| Day 3 (first orders) | Reel: first pickup code | "First order just went through" | "First order placed on uTap at NMU. Pickup code worked." | — |
| Day 4 (student recruitment) | Reel: NFC card scan | "Students scanning their cards" | Thread: "What we learned on day 3 of the NMU launch" | — |
| Day 5 (wrap) | Carousel: "NMU Alpha is live" | "We're going home. Here's what happened." | Thread: "5 days in PE. Here's what we learned." | "NMU Alpha week 1 — what happened" |

**Content rule for launch week:** Raw and honest beats polished. A shaky 20-second clip of the first order going through is worth more than a produced video. Authenticity is the content.

## Phase 6 — Ongoing Weekly Cadence (Post-Launch)

Once the Alpha is running and the team is back in Joburg, the sustainable weekly rhythm is:

**Monday:** Post 1 — data or insight post on Instagram or LinkedIn (whoever's week it is)
**Thursday:** Post 2 — student-facing content on Instagram or TikTok

**Plus daily (15 minutes total):**
- Reply to 2–3 comments or DMs
- Engage with 3 relevant accounts on X

That is it. Two posts and 15 minutes of engagement per day. Anything more is a bonus, not a requirement.

## Phase 7 — Metrics to Track

Do not optimise for follower count. Optimise for engagement rate and link clicks in the first 3 months.

| Metric | What it tells you | Target (Month 1) | Target (Month 3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram reach per post | Is the content getting seen beyond followers? | 500+ | 2,000+ |
| TikTok views per video | Is the content resonating? | 1,000+ | 5,000+ |
| X impressions per thread | Are the threads landing? | 2,000+ | 10,000+ |
| LinkedIn post reactions | Is the vendor/university audience engaging? | 20+ | 100+ |
| Link in bio clicks (Instagram) | Are social followers converting to site visitors? | 50/month | 200/month |
| App downloads from social | Are social followers converting to users? | Track from Week 4 | 50+ |

**Monthly review (30 minutes):** Look at what performed best, do more of that. Look at what got zero engagement, stop doing that. No complex analytics needed at this stage — just pattern recognition.

## Phase 8 — What Not to Do

These are the mistakes that kill early-stage social accounts. Avoid all of them.

**Do not post without a point of view.** "Happy Monday! Have a great week 🎉" is brand noise. Every post should say something specific about campus life, vendor economics, or the product.

**Do not use too many hashtags.** 5–8 on Instagram. 0–2 on X. 0 on LinkedIn. More looks desperate and signals low-quality content to algorithms.

**Do not post and disappear.** Reply to every comment in the first hour. The algorithm surfaces posts that generate replies quickly. Ignoring comments kills reach.

**Do not post the same content on every platform.** Instagram gets the visual. X gets the insight. LinkedIn gets the business case. TikTok gets the raw moment. Same story, different angle.

**Do not chase trends that have nothing to do with uTap.** Using an unrelated trending audio or meme format to get views trains the wrong audience to follow you. Every follower who does not care about campus life is a follower who will not convert to a user.

**Do not buy followers.** Ever. It destroys engagement rate, which makes organic reach worse, not better.

---

## The Single Most Important Social Rule for uTap

> **Students follow accounts that feel like they get campus life. Vendors follow accounts that tell them how to make more money. Never confuse the two audiences in the same post.**

Student content: Instagram + TikTok. Raw, relatable, campus-native.
Vendor content: LinkedIn + X. Economics, margins, operational insight.

The brand voice is the same — direct, warm, honest. The angle changes by platform.
