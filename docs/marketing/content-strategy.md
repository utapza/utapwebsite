# uTap Content Strategy

*Created: May 2026 | Phase: Pre-launch → NMU Pilot*

---

## Strategic Context

**Primary goal:** Drive student installs and vendor sign-ups through organic search and shareable campus content — before the NMU pilot launches.

**Content priorities (in order):**
1. **Searchable** — capture students and vendors already searching for alternatives to delivery apps and cashless campus solutions
2. **Shareable** — build brand awareness on campus-adjacent social channels (X, TikTok, student Facebook groups)
3. **Authority** — establish uTap as the credible voice on campus commerce in South Africa

**Resources:** Small team (3 people). Prioritise low-lift, high-searchability formats first. No video initially.

---

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Campus Ordering & Delivery Fees 🎯
*The core acquisition pillar — captures high-intent students already frustrated with delivery costs.*

**Hub:** "Campus food ordering in South Africa — what students actually pay"

**Spoke cluster:**
- "uTap vs Mr D: the real cost of ordering lunch on campus"
- "How much do South African students spend on delivery fees per month?"
- "Why delivery apps don't work between lectures"
- "The best campus food apps in South Africa (2026)"
- "How to skip the queue at your campus café"

**Connects to product:** uShop zero-markup ordering. Primary conversion → student app download.

---

### Pillar 2: Campus Vendor Economics 💰
*Captures vendors searching for delivery platform alternatives. High commercial intent.*

**Hub:** "Campus food vendor guide: how to increase margins and reach students"

**Spoke cluster:**
- "Mr D commission: what campus vendors actually pay (and keep)"

- "Campus food vendor marketing: how to reach students without a delivery app"

- "How to manage food orders at a campus tuckshop"

**Connects to product:** Vendor Starter plan → Growth subscription. Primary conversion → vendor sign-up.

---

### Pillar 3: Student Life & Campus Finance 📱
*Broad awareness content. Shareable. Builds brand with students before they have a specific need.*

**Hub:** "The complete guide to managing money as a South African student"

**Spoke cluster:**
- "How to save money at university in South Africa"
- "Student budgeting tips: how to make NSFAS last longer"
- "The hidden costs of university life in South Africa"

**Connects to product:** Student freemium tier. NFC wallet. Primary conversion → app download.

---

### Pillar 4: Campus Access & Student ID Technology 🔑
*Niche but low-competition. Captures students/IT admins searching about NFC and card technology.*

**Hub:** "Student NFC cards in South Africa — how they work and what's possible"

**Spoke cluster:**
- "How NFC student cards work (and why your phone can replace yours)"

**Connects to product:** NFC wallet — the core hook. Primary conversion → early access sign-up.

---

*Builds brand authority and shareability. Targets campus administrators, press, and ecosystem builders.*

## Distribution Plan Per Piece

Every blog post published should be repurposed as:

1. **X/Twitter thread** — lead with the most surprising stat, link back to post
2. **LinkedIn post** — professional angle for vendor/university audience
3. **WhatsApp status / campus group share** — student-voice angle, informal
4. **Email to captured leads** — cheat sheet subscribers get new blog roundup monthly
5. **Internal links** — every post links to `/vs/mr-d`, `/vs/eezipay`, or `/downloads/campus-ordering`
