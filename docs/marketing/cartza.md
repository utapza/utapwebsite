# CartZA Competitive Analysis & Research Findings

## Executive Summary

CartZA is a student-focused campus food ordering and delivery platform currently operating primarily around the University of the Free State (UFS) in Bloemfontein.

The platform positions itself strongly around:

- student convenience
- queue-skipping
- campus delivery
- hyper-local commerce

Their branding and niche positioning are compelling and targeted, particularly around university life and student pain points.

However, despite strong positioning, significant weaknesses were identified across:

- onboarding UX
- operational transparency
- product polish
- scalability
- vendor tooling
- platform maturity
- execution consistency

The platform currently appears to function more as:

- a lightweight campus delivery coordination marketplace

rather than:

- a deeply integrated merchant operating platform.

---

# 1. Company Positioning & Brand Strategy

## Core Positioning

CartZA brands itself as:

> “Built by students, for students.”

The platform positions itself specifically around:

- campus food ordering
- student convenience
- residence delivery
- queue elimination

This focused positioning is one of their strongest strategic advantages.

---

# 2. Problem They Are Solving

CartZA frames its solution around real student pain points:

- standing in long food queues
- missing meals between lectures
- walking long distances for food
- limited time between classes
- residence convenience

This messaging is effective because it is:

- relatable
- operationally grounded
- highly targeted

---

# 3. Operational Model

## Business Model

CartZA appears to operate primarily as:

- a commission-based marketplace/intermediary.

### Revenue Flow

1. Customer places order
2. CartZA coordinates order
3. Vendor fulfills order
4. Delivery partner delivers order
5. CartZA earns commission from vendor after successful sale

This indicates they monetize through:

- successful transaction commissions
  rather than:
- subscriptions
- SaaS tooling
- vendor software licensing

# 6. Major UX & Onboarding Problems

## Broken Geo-Location Flow

The onboarding process heavily depends on map-based coordinate selection.

### Problems Identified

- Map interaction unreliable/non-clickable
- Search suggestions visually cut off
- Coordinates not auto-filling
- Latitude/longitude fields locked as readonly
- Required manual browser inspection to bypass restrictions

---

## Poor Validation UX

An image upload error stated:

> “Cover photo must not be greater than 2048 kilobytes”

However:

- no upload limit guidance existed beforehand
- no helper text was shown
- validation only appeared after submission

---

## Confusing “Out of Zone” Errors

Signup repeatedly failed with:

> “Geo coordinates out of zone”

Issues:

- no explanation of supported service area
- unclear meaning of “zone”
- operational restrictions hidden from users

Only later did research reveal:
the platform currently supports vendors near UFS/Bloemfontein.

---

# 7. Student Verification Findings

## Contradiction Between Policy & Product

CartZA’s privacy policy references:

- student ID verification

However:

- no student verification was requested during app usage
- no student ID was required during onboarding or ordering

This suggests:

- the feature may be unimplemented
- verification may be administrative only
- the platform relies more on geographic restriction than actual student validation

---

# 8. Vendor Infrastructure Findings

Vendors appear to:

- continue operating independently

- advanced vendor portals
- merchant analytics
- inventory systems
- deep business tooling
- operational management systems
