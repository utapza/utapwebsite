# Audience: utap.co.za (Marketing Site)

The marketing site (`utapwebsiite`) is the front door of uTap on the open web. It speaks to **two audiences in two voices**:

1. **Students** considering downloading the app.
2. **Vendors** considering opening a store.

Plus a small layer of **partners** (universities, sponsors, press) we route via dedicated pages.

---

## 1. Visitors in one paragraph

A student browses on their phone, often from a friend's link or a search after seeing a uTap sticker on a café till. They want a quick answer: "what is this?". They make up their mind in five seconds. A vendor browses on a laptop after a sales call or a referral, with a budget and a specific question: "how much, how soon, how do I get paid?". They make up their mind in three minutes.

Both audiences will judge uTap on whether the page **proves** what it promises — vague badges and stock photos lose them.

---

## 2. Personas

### Student visitor

- 18–22, mobile-first, often arrives via Instagram, TikTok, or a friend.
- Wants: a 5-second answer plus the App Store / Google Play link.
- Will scroll if the hero hooks them, especially with a real screenshot.
- Will leave if the page feels like a generic SaaS site.

### Vendor visitor

- 25–55, desktop-first, often arrives from a sales conversation or a search.
- Wants: how it works, what it costs, who else uses it, how to apply.
- Will read a longer page if it answers their questions in order.
- Will leave if pricing is hidden behind "contact sales" with no real explanation.

### University / partner visitor

- Comms or facilities lead at a university.
- Wants: who runs uTap, what data students share, what privacy commitments are in place.
- Will read carefully. Treat them with formality without sounding stuffy.

---

## 3. Top jobs the site does

1. Convince a student to download the app.
2. Convince a vendor to start the signup flow.
3. Reassure partners that uTap is a safe, professional operation.
4. Answer common questions before support is needed.
5. Drive press and brand searches to a confident About page.

---

## 4. Tone for utap.co.za

- **Confident and concrete.** Real numbers, real screenshots, real vendor names where allowed.
- **Verb-led.** Headlines start with what the visitor does, not who we are.
- **South African.** Reference real campuses, real foods, real weather (matric finals, rugby season, winter coffee runs).
- **Pickup-first language.** Never imply we're a delivery platform.
- **No marketing soup.** No "leverage", "synergy", "platform of choice".

---

## 6. Anti-personas

- Visitors looking for a tracking app for their child.
- Companies looking to sell to students en masse.
- Job seekers (we link to a careers page if hiring; otherwise we don't pretend).
