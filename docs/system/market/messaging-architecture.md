# uTap Messaging Architecture

How the positioning turns into headlines, taglines, and value props you can drop straight into a landing page, an ad, an app store screenshot, or an investor deck.

This file is for **anyone writing public-facing copy**. Internal UI copy lives in the per-app copy specs.

---

## 1. Master tagline

> **Tap in. Order up. Show up.**

Three verbs, three jobs. Use this on:

- The marketing site hero.
- The app store subtitle.
- The launch campaign.
- The intro screen of the mobile app's onboarding.

### Variations (same DNA, different lengths)

| Length | Line |
|--------|------|
| 3 words | **Tap in. Done.** |
| 6 words | **Tap in. Order up. Show up.** |
| 9 words | **Your student card, your campus, your tickets — one tap.** |
| Long | **Tap into your campus, order from trusted vendors, and show up to games and events — all in one app.** |

Don't translate this into stiff English ("Tap-in functionality, order-up capability, etc."). The rhythm is the brand.

## 3. The three pillars

Every public message ladders to one of these three. If you can't tell which pillar a piece of copy belongs to, it's drifting.

### Pillar 1 — **The card on your phone** (Identity & Access)

**Promise:** Your student card lives on your phone so you can tap into the places you go every day.

**Sub-messages:**

- "Scan once. Tap forever."
- "Backed up to the cloud, restored when you log in."
- "Works with the card you already have."
- "No more replacing it at admin."

**Audience:** Every student is the audience. This is the broadest pillar — also the strongest hook for first install.

---

### Pillar 2 — **The campus you can order from** (Commerce — uShop)

**Promise:** Order from real campus vendors, pay on your phone, and skip the queue.

**Sub-messages:**

- "Cafés, bookshops, electronics, prints — all in one app."
- "Order on the way. Pick up the moment you arrive."
- "Pay with Yoco — Apple Pay, Google Pay, or your card."
- "Real menus, real stores, real prices."

**Audience:** Hungry students between lectures; vendors looking for new orders.

---

### Pillar 3 — **The events you don't miss** (Events — uGig)

**Promise:** Buy tickets for varsity games, society launches, and campus gigs on the same screen.

**Sub-messages:**

- "Game day, sorted."
- "Tickets that live with your card."
- "Tap to show, no print-outs."
- "Gear and merch when you grab the ticket."

**Audience:** Sports clubs, society leaders, students who actually go out.

## 5. Headline framework (for new pages)

When writing a new landing page, ad, or campaign, follow this template.

```
[ Hook: a verb the audience already does ]
[ Outcome: what they get in one line ]
[ Proof: a specific thing only uTap can claim ]
[ CTA: a verb that names what happens next ]
```

### Example (student landing page)

```
Hook:     Tap in. Order up. Show up.
Outcome:  Your student card, your campus food, and your event tickets — one app.
Proof:    Built for South African campuses with trusted vendors and Yoco payments.
CTA:      Download uTap
```

### Example (vendor landing page)

```
Hook:     Sell to students before they walk past.
Outcome:  Take orders from the campus next door — paid via Yoco, ready when they arrive.
Proof:    Used by [X] campus vendors across [Y] universities.
CTA:      Open my store
```

## 6. Tone shifts across the funnel

The same product speaks differently at different moments.

| Funnel stage | Tone | Example |
|--------------|------|---------|
| Awareness ad | Playful hook | "That feeling when you forgot your student card. Again." |
| Landing page hero | Confident promise | "Tap in. Order up. Show up." |
| Feature page | Specific and proof-led | "Scan once. We back it up to the cloud so you can restore it on any phone." |
| App store listing | Bullet outcomes | "Tap into buildings · Order food · Get tickets" |
| Signup screen | Reassuring | "Let's get your student card on your phone." |
| Welcome email | Warm and useful | "Welcome to uTap. Here's how to scan your card." |
| Activation push | Specific nudge | "You're 30 seconds from tapping in. Scan your card now." |
| Lapsed user email | Curious, not guilt-trippy | "We've added stores at your campus since you last tapped in." |

## 8. Forbidden positioning language

Never frame uTap as:

- "Disrupting" anything.
- "The Uber of [thing]." (We are pickup-first; the metaphor breaks.)
- "AI-powered." (We don't sell AI.)
- "Revolutionising student life." (Cliché.)
- "All-in-one super-app." (Sounds like an empty wrapper.)
- "The future of [campus / payments / identity]." (Future-talk over-promises.)

If a piece of copy reads like a generic SaaS hero, send it back.

---

## 9. Approval rules for new headlines

| Change | Approver |
|--------|----------|
| Headline variation within an existing pillar | Marketing lead |
| New pillar or shift in master tagline | Marketing lead + Founder |
| Claim involving money, security, or numbers | Marketing + Finance / Engineering |

If a headline makes a quantitative claim ("over 10 000 students", "saves R 300 a month"), the number has to be auditable. No round-number lies.
