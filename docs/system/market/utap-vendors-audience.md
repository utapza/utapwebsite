# Audience: uTap for Vendors

The vendor portal (`utap-vendors`) is the operational console for campus store operators. This doc captures **who the vendor is**, **what they're trying to get done**, and **how to talk to them**.

---

## 1. The vendor in one paragraph

A small-business owner or campus shop manager running a store near a South African university. They sell food, snacks, books, prints, electronics, stationery, or sports gear. They already have a queue at lunchtime and a basic till, and they want more orders without hiring more people. They sign in from a phone or tablet between rushes; they don't have time for software that talks at them.

---

## 2. Personas

### Persona A — **Sibusiso, owner of Campus Café**

- 34, owns a food truck and a sit-down café near a residence.
- Took over the shop 18 months ago. Doing okay but wants more.
- Uses a Yoco card machine; familiar with Yoco's app.
- Has 4 staff. Manages WhatsApp orders himself, badly.
- Phone-first. Opens a laptop maybe once a week.
- **What he needs:** more order volume, fewer chats, payouts he can predict.

### Persona B — **Riana, manager of a campus bookshop chain**

- 41, manages three bookshops at three nearby campuses.
- Spends mornings on stock, afternoons on sales reports.
- Has back-office staff who handle accounting.
- Comfortable on a laptop. Used Shopify before; found it overkill for campus.
- **What she needs:** multi-store view, clean order history, sensible payouts.

### Persona C — **Lebo, student running a side hustle**

- 23, sells stationery and graduation gear out of a small kiosk her parents helped her start.
- Single-store operator. Often closes early when class runs late.
- Uses Instagram for marketing; doesn't have a website.
- **What she needs:** a simple way to take orders that doesn't require her to be available 24/7.

## 3. Vendor jobs-to-be-done

| Job | When it happens | How uTap shows up |
|-----|------------------|---------------------|
| Open a store on uTap | Day one of joining | Vendor signup → store setup |
| List products and prices | First week, then ongoing | Product list and editor |
| Accept new orders | All day during opening hours | Order inbox with sound + push |
| Manage prep time | At lunch and dinner rush | Set realistic ETAs per order or globally |
| Mark order ready | Once cooked / packed | One-tap status updates |
| Hand over the order | Student arrives | Show the pickup code |
| Update stock | Daily / weekly | Stock toggle, "sold out" quick action |
| Run a special | Promo periods | Vendor-funded promotions |
| Get paid | End of month | Yoco payouts on a schedule |
| Add another shop | When they expand | Multi-store support |

---

## 4. Vendor pain points

1. **WhatsApp order chaos.** Different chats, no audit trail, missed orders.
2. **Cash float and reconciliation.** Counting at end-of-day.
3. **Surprise payouts.** Not knowing when money lands.
4. **Telling students they're sold out.** Awkward over chat; clean in an app.
5. **Promotions that don't reach the right students.** Posters and Instagram aren't enough.
6. **Setup nightmares.** Most "platform" tools want an hour of setup.

## 5. Emotional drivers

| What they feel | When | We respond by |
|----------------|------|----------------|
| Pressure | Lunch rush | Calm, operational copy. Big buttons. |
| Pride | First R 10 000 month | Celebrate milestones in the dashboard. |
| Cautious | Adding a payment method | Reassure with Yoco's name and concrete numbers. |
| Frustrated | An order they can't fulfil | Clear cancel/refund flows; no shame. |
| Curious | New feature drops | Short release notes; not novella-length. |

---

## 6. Tone for the vendor portal

- **Calm.** No exclamation marks. Vendors are mid-shift.
- **Operational.** Show numbers, statuses, and times. Don't be cute.
- **Respectful of their time.** Short labels. Default sensible behaviours. Confirm only when stakes are real.
- **Honest about money.** Always show the fee breakdown. Never round in our favour.

The student app is warm and a little playful. The vendor portal is the **calm sibling**.

---

## 7. Language accessibility

- Reading level: **Grade 10**. Vendors are professionals; we can use words like "payout", "VAT", "stock".
- Avoid jargon unique to e-commerce platforms ("SKU", "PIM", "AOV"). Translate to plain language ("product code", "average order").
- South African English: "stock-take", "till", "cheque", "EFT", "VAT".

## 8. Channels vendors live on

| Channel | Use case |
|---------|----------|
| The vendor portal itself | Day-to-day operations |
| Email | Onboarding, payout statements, monthly summary |
| WhatsApp Business | Onboarding support, critical alerts (opt-in) |
| Phone | Escalation only |
| In-portal updates | Release notes, new features |

We don't push vendors via SMS unless something material happens (e.g. a payout was sent).

---

## 9. What vendors don't want from us

- Constant "engagement" emails.
- A monthly e-book about "growing your business".
- Feature releases they have to opt into manually for each store.
- Surprise pricing changes.
- A redesign that moves where the "new orders" badge lives.

---

## 10. High-anxiety moments for vendors

These are the moments where the portal's calm tone matters most:

1. **The first day live.** They're nervous a student will order and they'll fumble.
2. **Receiving a refund request.** Money out feels worse than money in feels good.
3. **Reading their first payout statement.** Will the numbers add up?
4. **Closing the shop early.** Communicating to in-progress orders.
5. **Suspending a product mid-order.** What happens to the student?

---

## 11. Vendor signup pipeline

This is the only "marketing inside the product" moment for vendors. Handle with care.

1. **Discover uTap** (via student demand, sales rep, word of mouth, marketing site).
2. **Sign up** — name, email, store name, university/campus.
3. **Set up store** — store type, hours, logo, description.
4. **Add bank details** — Yoco account or new bank account.
5. **List first 5 products** (we recommend a starter set).
6. **Go live** — flip the switch.

The vendor copy doc gives the exact strings.

---

## 12. Anti-personas

- **Vendors not based on or near a campus.** uTap is campus commerce.
- **Mass-market chains running corporate marketing campaigns.** Yes to a Pick n Pay express store on campus; no to launching a non-campus campaign through us.
- **Anyone needing a full POS system.** We complement; we don't replace tills.
- **Online-only sellers.** We're for pickup-first physical stores.
